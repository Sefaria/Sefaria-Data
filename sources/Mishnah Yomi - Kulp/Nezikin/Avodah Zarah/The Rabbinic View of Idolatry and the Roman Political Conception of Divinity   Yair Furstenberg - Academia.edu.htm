i. between bible and mishna
The biblical demand for a struggle against the worship of idols is zeal-ous, even violent in nature; its aim is the eradication of all alternativesto the God of Israel. This struggle, derived from the uniqueness of God,is translated in the Bible into resolute, decisive action, as exempliedin Deuteronomy 12: You must destroy all the sites, at which the nations you are to dispossess worshiped their gods, whether on lofty mountainsand on hills or under any luxuriant tree. Tear down their altars, smashtheir pillars, put their sacred posts to the re, and cut down the imagesof their gods, obliterating their name from that site (12:23).
