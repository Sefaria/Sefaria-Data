/SCM/XXEDITATION/XX/PR
On the second day of Pesah/± in ancient times, our ancestors
brought the first sheaf of barley reaped that season as an offering
to God. From that day, they began counting the days and weeks
to Shavuot, when they would celebrate the beginning of the
wheat harvest by offering the loaves made of the first wheat.
Even after the Temple was destroyed and offerings were no
longer brought, they continued to count the days from Pesah/± to
Shavuot in accordance with the biblical injunction./PR
