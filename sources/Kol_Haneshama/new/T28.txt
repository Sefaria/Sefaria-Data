/SCN/XXOTE/XX. Traditional siddurim place an additional (/ITMusaf/XX) /ITAmidah/XX after returning the Torah to the ark. The /ITMusaf Amidah/XX corresponds to the additional sacrifice that was offered in the Temple on Shabbat and Festivals. Because Reconstructionists do not anticipate or hope for the rebuilding of the Temple, we do not feel a strong need to retain its liturgical rhythms or emphasis on animal sacrifice. This siddur therefore omits the /ITMusaf Amidah/XX for the sake of brevity. /ITYismeh/±u/XX, a joyfully sung part of the /ITMusaf Amidah/XX, is included in the /ITShah/±arit Amidah/XX here (page 309). Many congregations recite poetry in the place of the /ITMusaf Amidah/XX as well. Those wishing to recite a full /ITMusaf Amidah/XX may use the separate booklet prepared for that purpose or turn back to the Shabbat /ITAmidah/XX found on pages 294-323, omitting page 307 or replacing it with a reading from pages 734-739.
