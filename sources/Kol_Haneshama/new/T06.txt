The language and manner of reciting the <i>Tefilah</i> offer insights intothe place of the individual in communal prayer. When the <i>Tefilah</i> is recitedprivately, each individual stands (hence the name <i>Amidah</i>, "standing") andaddresses God in a sustained conversational way. By concentrating on examples of heal-ing forces and life-sustaining rains, the second <i>beraḥah</i> acknowledges Godas the power that sustains life. The traditional emphasis on God's abilityto resurrect the dead has been replaced here by a celebration of God as thepower that sustains all life.
